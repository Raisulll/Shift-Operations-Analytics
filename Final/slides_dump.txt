[TEXT] It's not a charting task — it's a trust-the-data task
[TEXT] Shift Operations Analytics  ·  Md Raisul Islam Rahad
[TEXT] 01 / 21
[TEXT] Given shift records with deliberate operational inconsistencies, clean the data, visualize shift patterns, score efficiency, detect recurring breakdown periods, and surface actionable insights — and stay useful when new categories appear or are grouped.
[TEXT] Dirty data
Invalid dates, bad/missing timestamps, negative & mismatched hours, duplicates, overnight shifts — silently dropping rows hides the truth.
[TEXT] Meaningful analysis
Efficiency, multi-day streaks, reliability — each needs a defensible definition, not just a number.
[TEXT] Extensibility
No reason list can be hardcoded; grouping must re-flow every chart, streak and score.
[NOTES] The assignment isn't about drawing charts — it's about handling data you can't trust and building believable analysis on top of it. Those three challenges drove every design decision.

[TEXT] 06 · REQUIREMENT 3 · SHIFT CHART
[TEXT] THE BRIEF, DELIVERED LIVE
[TEXT] A 36-hour axis keeps overnight shifts as one continuous bar
[TEXT] Shift Operations Analytics  ·  Md Raisul Islam Rahad
[TEXT] 06 / 21
[TEXT] Date on X, time of day on Y, one coloured bar per shift. A 22:00 → 06:00 shift crosses midnight, so the axis runs 12 AM → next 12 PM (36 h): an overnight bar simply passes the 24-hour line — see Oct 4 — instead of wrapping or breaking.
[NOTES] A subtle correctness issue that's easy to get wrong. Real plants run night shifts, so I extended the axis to 36 hours. A bar's start and end are just minutes from midnight, so a cross-midnight shift naturally draws as one continuous bar past the 24-hour mark instead of wrapping around.

[TEXT] Shift Operations Analytics  ·  Md Raisul Islam Rahad

[TEXT] ✓  Timestamps are internally consistent  —  and they drive the timeline / chart axis anyway.
[TEXT] ✓  HOURS is a single scalar  —  — the easy field to fumble on manual data entry.
[TEXT] ✓  Documented, defensible assumption  —  written in the README and the code, not a silent choice.
[TEXT] 📌  A decision I'd flag to a stakeholder, not bury in code — if HOURS were authoritative it's a one-function change. And the data model keeps the audit trail: every row stores its raw values, recomputed fields, is_valid flag and issue list.
[NOTES] This is a judgement call and I made it deliberately. I measured it — 44 of 51 rows already agree with the timestamps — so treating timestamps as authoritative recovers the mismatched rows instead of trusting an easily mistyped number. And I documented it.

[TEXT] APPENDIX · Q&A PREP
[TEXT] Anticipated questions — architecture & data
[TEXT] Shift Operations Analytics  ·  Md Raisul Islam Rahad
[TEXT] A1
[TEXT] Q  Why put all analysis on the backend, not the client?
A  One source of truth, testable in isolation, reusable by any client, and heavy pandas work stays off the browser. Trade-off: a round-trip per interaction — negligible at this scale.
[TEXT] Q  Why SQLite locally but Postgres in production?
A  Zero-setup dev vs durable, concurrent prod. dj-database-url picks the engine from DATABASE_URL — identical models and migrations, no code change.
[TEXT] Q  What happens when HOURS and the timestamps disagree?
A  Timestamps win — HOURS is recomputed. One explicit rule resolves a whole class of conflicts consistently, and it’s configurable if the opposite were wanted.
[TEXT] Q  How do you handle a brand-new failure reason?
A  It flows through automatically — reasons are derived from data, colours are deterministic, grouping and policy live in config. Demoed by sample_new_categories.

[TEXT] Q&A · SCALE, TESTING & AI
[TEXT] APPENDIX · Q&A PREP
[TEXT] Anticipated questions — scale, testing & AI
[TEXT] Shift Operations Analytics  ·  Md Raisul Islam Rahad
[TEXT] A2
[TEXT] Q  How would this scale to millions of rows?
A  Move cleaning to a batch / streaming job, store aggregates, and paginate. The clean separation — pipeline → stored records → analysis — means the API and UI wouldn’t change.
[TEXT] Q  What stops the AI features from fabricating numbers?
A  The model only sees pre-computed metrics, never raw rows; grouping output is validated against the real reason list. Without a key the features disable cleanly.
[TEXT] Q  How is the cross-midnight shift handled correctly?
A  The backend emits minutes-from-midnight (end can exceed 1440) and a crosses_midnight flag; the chart uses a 36-hour axis so an overnight shift is one continuous bar.
[TEXT] Q  What would you improve with more time?
A  Auth & per-user datasets, background jobs for large uploads, caching of analysis responses, and end-to-end (Playwright) tests over the existing coverage.
